Customer & Relationship Management

Managing your customer base is the heartbeat of the distribution network. This section guides you through creating highly detailed customer profiles.

How to Add a New Customer

1
Initialize Modal Navigate to the Customers view and click the + Add Customer button.
2
Basic Identity Enter the Legal Name of the shop or individual. Use the **Category** dropdown to tag them as a 'Retailer', 'Distributor', or 'Corporate'.
3
Geographical Routing Select a **Route**. This is vital as it determines when this customer will appear in specific truck load workflows.
Visual Interface: Automated Customer Onboarding Form
Pro Discovery: If you enter an Opening Balance, the system automatically creates a virtual ledger entry to track this legacy credit.

Supply Chain Master Modules

Ensure your procurement flows are tracked by setting up Suppliers, Brands, and Categories correctly.

1. Adding a Strategic Supplier

1
Supplier Registry Go to Inventory > Suppliers. Fill in the Primary Contact and Address.
2
Management Hierarchy You can tag the Area Manager Name and TSR Name for each supplier to ensure account accountability.

2. Organizing Brands & Categories

Brand Master

Group products by their manufacturer (e.g., Bayer, Syngenta). Click Manage Brands at the top of the Inventory page to add new ones.

Category Control

Categorize items by chemical class (e.g., Insecticides, Herbicides). This enables high-intensity reporting on market trends.

Field Distribution Strategy

This module optimizes how finished goods leave the warehouse and reach the doorstep of the customer.

How to Create a New Truck Load

1
Asset Selection Choose an active truck from your fleet. Ensure the registration number matches the physical vehicle being loaded.
2
Inventory Allocation Add products to the loading table. The system will show your current **Warehouse Stock**. You cannot load more than what is physically available in the main store.
Operational View: Inventory Allocation for Mobile Distribution Units

Next-Gen POS (Invoicing)

The Point of Sale is designed for zero-latency billing. It uses a dynamic grid system for lightning-fast product entry.

Initiating a High-Volume Invoice

  • 1
    Identify Customer: Search by name or phone. The system instantly reveals their credit worthiness.
  • 2
    Stock Source: Select "Main Warehouse" for local sales or a "Truck Load ID" for field sales.
  • 3
    Line Item Entry: Scan barcodes or click on the product cards. The unit price will auto-populate based on the last transaction with this client.
Intelligence Layer: Interactive Billing & Checkout Interface
Security Protocol: Invoices marked as **'Split'** allow for partial payment (e.g., $500 Cash + $1000 Account Credit). The system handles the complex double-entry bookkeeping automatically.

System Integrity & Maintenance

Keep your ERP running at peak performance with periodic maintenance tasks.

Backup Protocol

Go to **Settings > Backups**. It is recommended to download a full database dump every 24 hours.

Audit Intelligence

Review the **System Logs** weekly to identify unauthorized changes or investigate user errors.
